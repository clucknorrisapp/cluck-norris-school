To the Solana Foundation grants team,

Thank you for the feedback the first time around, and for pointing us toward other programs and places that might be a fit — I mean that genuinely, and I followed the links. You also mentioned we'd be welcome to reapply once things had changed for us. They have — a lot — so here I am.

Not a redirect, not a side program. With you. That's why I'd rather build this with the people whose mission it actually is.

## Why I built all of this on Solana

I didn't pick Solana because it was convenient. I picked it because it's where the people I want to help actually show up — and where they actually get hurt. The retail wave arriving through Solana right now, much of it through launchpads and memecoins, is bringing first-time users on-chain by the thousands, and most of them have no idea what a token approval is, what a rug looks like, or how to read a contract before they buy. That isn't a knock on Solana. It's the exact reason to build a school here. You go where the newcomers are.

And Solana is the only chain where this school could work the way I wanted it to. Crypto safety can't be taught from a slide deck — people learn by doing. On Solana, a learner with five dollars can make a wallet, do a real swap, run a real holder snapshot, lock real tokens, and scan a real wallet for the drainer approvals that quietly empty people — all for fractions of a cent. You cannot teach hands-on, learn-by-doing crypto on a chain where every practice transaction costs twenty dollars. Cheap, fast finality is what turns "here's how it works" into "now try it yourself." Even the way our tools pay for themselves — a unique-decimal token transfer the server verifies on-chain, with no wallet-connect, no custody, and no signature request — only exists because Solana made that pattern cheap and instant.

## Why I want to educate everyone — specifically on Solana

Because I see the Foundation's vision as more than a chain. Onboarding the next wave of users and making crypto usable for normal people is, almost word for word, what this school is for. When Solana leaned into mobile and the Seeker, it was meeting the next users where they already are — on their phones — and we followed you there: the school is live on the Seeker dApp Store, putting free crypto-safety education directly on the device.

Everything we build is token-agnostic and free — the Token Autopsy, the Wallet X-Ray, the holder-snapshot tool, the Wallet Safety Checkup, and now the Locker Room, a non-custodial, no-token-cut way for any Solana project to lock its tokens and for any holder to verify it on-chain. None of it is a funnel for our own token. It's infrastructure for the whole ecosystem, because the safer and healthier Solana is for a newcomer, the better it is for everyone building here. It's already live in seven languages and counting, and it's all open source under the MIT license, so any Solana builder can fork the curriculum, the forensic engine, or the no-wallet-connect payment pattern.

I want to keep building this for years — for the person who just made their first wallet and doesn't want to lose everything in week one. I believe that's your mission too. That's why I'd rather do it with the Foundation than anywhere else.
