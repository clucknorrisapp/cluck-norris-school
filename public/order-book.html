Try: CLKN WIF JUP
❓ How to read this — plain English
Limit order / wall — a buy or sell order someone parked at a set price, waiting for the market to reach it. A big sell wall above the price acts as resistance (price has to chew through it to go up); a big buy wall below acts as support.
Liquidity depth / price impact — how far the price moves when someone trades a given size. If a $5,000 buy moves the price +30%, liquidity is thin (easy to move); if it barely moves, it's deep. This is measured across every pool at once (via Jupiter routing).
Single‑sided Orca position — on Orca, a one‑sided liquidity position acts exactly like a limit order: it sells (or buys) as the price passes through it. We read these directly and show who owns them.
Yours vs 3rd‑party — put your LP wallet(s) in the box above and any wall you own is tagged ✓ yours; anything else is ⚠ 3rd‑party — the stuff worth watching.
Sources scanned: Jupiter limit orders (v1+v2), cross‑pool AMM depth, and Orca single‑sided walls. Empty results are normal for thin tokens — orders often appear and fill within seconds (the monitor catches those).
