⚙️ HOW IT WORKS · CONCENTRATED LIQUIDITY, AUTOMATED

Built on Orca Whirlpools (Solana concentrated liquidity). Instead of spreading capital thin across every possible price, it places deep liquidity in a focused range around the current price — so trades fill tight and you earn the fee on every swap that passes through.

The three shapes of depth

  • Balanced base — two-sided depth straddling the price: bids below, asks above. The everyday market — smooth buys and sells.
  • Sell-side wall (single-sided) — your token's asks stacked above price, in a premium fee tier. You sell into strength at a fat fee when buyers pump in.
  • Buy-side support (single-sided) — bids below price that catch dips and let you accumulate.

It runs itself

1Re-centers automatically. As price moves, the position rolls to stay centered — your depth follows the market instead of going stale.
2Rebalances its own inventory. When one side runs low, it swaps (via Jupiter) to refund the pools — so it never goes one-sided and stops working. It never sells your token to do this.
3Buys back to replenish. As buyers take your token through the asks, USDC accumulates — and it can use that to buy the token back and reload the walls.
4Harvests the natural arbitrage. Run a pool in both token/USDC and token/SOL, and every move in SOL's price nudges them out of line — arbitrageurs trade them back together, paying you fees on volume the market generates for free.
The flywheel: real depth → real fills + real arb → fees + accumulated quote currency → rebalance & buy back → deeper depth. A self-sustaining loop powered by genuine market activity, not by you faking trades.

Guardrails, always on

Anti-thrash interval (no churning), daily action caps, a price-gap anomaly guard (sits out flash moves), slippage caps on every swap, per-fee-tier pools, and a one-flag kill switch. It's autonomous, not reckless.

🎓 REAL VOLUME vs WASH TRADING · KNOW THE DIFFERENCE

This is the part most projects get dangerously wrong, so we teach it plainly. It is not about how many wallets you use. It's about beneficial ownership and intent.

  • Wash trading = you (or wallets you control) buy and sell to yourself to create the appearance of activity. No real change of ownership. It's manipulation — illegal in most jurisdictions, filtered by aggregators, and a reputation killer. Splitting it across "different wallets" is exactly the structuring enforcement looks for; it doesn't make it legal.
  • Market making / liquidity provision = you post real depth and other people trade against it. You take genuine inventory risk and earn the spread/fees. Real counterparties, real ownership changes — real, honest volume.
  • Arbitrage = independent traders correct price differences across your pools (often driven by SOL's own moves). You don't manufacture the trades; the market does — you just provide the depth they fill.
The honest test: is there a real counterparty on the other side of each fill, taking the opposite risk? If yes, it's real liquidity. If the only party is you, it's wash trading. The Liquidity Engine is built so the answer is always yes — and you can prove it.
