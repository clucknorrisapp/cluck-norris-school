What is Solana?

Solana is a Layer-1 blockchain — a base network, like Bitcoin or Ethereum, that records transactions and runs programs (smart contracts). It launched in March 2020, built by Solana Labs (co-founded by Anatoly Yakovenko and Raj Gokal).

Its whole design goal is speed and low cost. Where older chains process a handful of transactions per second and can charge dollars in fees, Solana targets thousands of transactions per second with fees that are usually a small fraction of a cent. That's what makes it the home for high-frequency use cases: trading, payments, games, and the memecoin markets.

SOL is the network's native token. You use it to pay transaction fees, and you can stake it (lock it with a validator) to help secure the network and earn rewards.

How it works — Proof of History

Most blockchains slow down because every validator has to agree on when each transaction happened. Solana's key trick is Proof of History (PoH): a cryptographic clock that timestamps transactions before they reach consensus, so validators can process them in order without stopping to negotiate the time.

PoH sits on top of a Proof of Stake system (validators stake SOL to earn the right to produce blocks). Together they give Solana its ~400-millisecond block times and high throughput.

  • Fast: blocks finalize in well under a second.
  • Cheap: typical fees are tiny fractions of a cent.
  • Single global state: one fast chain, not many shards — simpler for apps.

The ecosystem

Solana's low fees made it a hub for on-chain finance and culture:

  • DeFi & trading: Jupiter (the routing layer), Orca, Raydium, Meteora — swap and provide liquidity.
  • Launchpads & memecoins: pump.fun, Bags — where new tokens are minted and traded.
  • Payments: Solana Pay and stablecoins (USDC) settle in under a second.
  • Mobile: the Saga and Seeker phones put a wallet in your pocket.

Fun fact: this very school's token, CLKN, lives on Solana — built on Bags, routed by Jupiter.

The honest risks

We don't shill here. If you're going to hold or use SOL, know the trade-offs:

  • Outage history: Solana suffered several network halts in 2021–2022 when overloaded. Reliability has improved a lot since, and a new validator client (Firedancer) aims to harden it further — but the track record is real.
  • Validator cost & concentration: running a validator takes serious hardware, which can concentrate who secures the network.
  • Early-backer baggage: FTX/Alameda were prominent early investors; their November 2022 collapse hammered SOL's price before it recovered. Association risk is part of the story.
  • Volatility & inflation: SOL's price swings hard, and new SOL is issued over time. Never invest money you can't afford to lose.

How to get started (safely)

  • Get a wallet: Phantom or Solflare. Write your seed phrase on paper — never type it into a website.
  • Get some SOL: from a reputable exchange, or swap into it. You'll need a little to pay fees.
  • Start tiny: send a small amount first to learn the flow before moving anything meaningful.
  • Verify everything: scams are everywhere. Learn to read a transaction before you sign it.
