3 · AUTHORITIES — recommended: revoke both
Revoke mint authority
After the full supply is minted, no more can ever be created. Supply is locked forever. Buyers look for this — it's the difference between a fixed token and one the dev can inflate.
Revoke freeze authority
No one can ever freeze a holder's tokens. Leaving freeze authority active is a common rug vector — revoking it removes that power entirely.
A third authority worth knowing — metadata (update authority).
Beyond mint and freeze there's the update authority: it controls whether your token's name, logo, and socials can be changed after launch. This minter leaves your metadata mutable on purpose — so you can fix a broken image or legitimately rebrand later. The tradeoff is real: a mutable token asks buyers to trust you won't bait-and-switch, while an immutable one locks the branding forever. Rug-checkers — including our own Token Autopsy — surface which one it is. If you'd rather lock yours permanently, you can set the metadata immutable or renounce the update authority after minting with a Metaplex tool (e.g. the Metaboss CLI). Neither choice is "right" — just know who can change what, because your holders will check.
📖 WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU MINT — THE LAUNCH LIQUIDITY GUIDE (read anytime)
A token is inert until liquidity is added — no price, no market, nothing to trade. This is what that next step involves. Read it before you do it: it's where real money is at stake, and it is not something to improvise. The Hatchery does not add liquidity for you — you do this yourself, on a DEX.
⚠️ Don't add a single dollar of liquidity until you understand all four sections below. The LP Lab goes deeper still.
1 · Starting ratio — this sets your opening price. To open a pool you deposit two things: your token and SOL (or USDC). The ratio of those amounts is the starting price — fewer tokens against more SOL opens higher, more tokens against less SOL opens lower. It is the single most consequential number in a launch. Decide the opening price you intend first, then work the deposit amounts backward from it.

ⓘ SOL vs. USDC pairing matters. If you pair with SOL, your token's price is set in SOL — so its dollar value rides SOL's: SOL up 20% and your untraded token is worth 20% more in USD; SOL down 20% and so is your token, even though nothing about it changed. Pairing with USDC anchors the price to the dollar instead. Neither is wrong — just know a SOL pair ties your token's USD value to SOL's swings.
2 · Standard pool vs. concentrated liquidity — the decision new creators get wrong most.
A standard pool (constant-product AMM) spreads your liquidity across every price from zero to infinity. Simple and forgiving — there is always liquidity at every price. Most tokens should launch this way.
A concentrated-liquidity pool lets you place liquidity only inside a chosen price range. Far more capital-efficient — but on a brand-new token it is a sharp tool that cuts you if mishandled:
• If price trades outside your range, your position becomes 100% one asset and stops earning fees — a too-tight range can leave a fresh token with effectively no liquidity within minutes of launch.
• With no price history, you're guessing the range blind. Too narrow and a normal early swing strands it; too wide and it behaves like a standard pool anyway.
• For a first launch: use a standard pool, or a concentrated pool with a very wide range. Tight ranges are a tool for managing an established token — not for opening a new one.
• Single-sided positions: some concentrated pools let you deposit only your token (no SOL) in a range above the current price — a staged sell wall. A deliberate strategy, never a default. Understand it fully first.
3 · Locking the liquidity. Whether you lock or burn the LP tokens you receive when the pool is created. Buyers check this — unlocked liquidity is the classic rug vector (the creator can pull it). Locking or burning is what signals you won't.
4 · Where to add it. Each DEX has its own pool creation flow — note which support concentrated liquidity:
• Meteora ↗ — DLMM pools are concentrated-liquidity; also has simpler dynamic pools. Popular for memecoin launches.
• Raydium ↗ — standard AMM pools plus concentrated (CLMM). The most widely integrated DEX on Solana.
• Orca ↗ — Whirlpools are concentrated-liquidity, with a clean, beginner-friendly interface.
