What is a token lock?

A lock takes tokens out of circulation and hands control to an on-chain program instead of a wallet. The tokens can only be released on a fixed, public schedule — nobody, not even the team, can pull them early. It’s the difference between a promise and a padlock: anyone can verify it on-chain.

01

Deposit

Tokens are sent into a vesting escrow you create — a program-owned vault, not a wallet.

02

Cliff

An optional waiting period before anything unlocks. Great for “nothing moves for 6 months.”

03

Vest

After the cliff, tokens release gradually over time — daily, weekly, monthly — until fully unlocked.

04

Claim

The recipient claims what has vested so far. Everything is public and verifiable, forever.

How Jupiter Lock works

No token cut, non-custodial

Jupiter Lock is an open-source, audited program (OtterSec & Sec3) that takes no cut of your tokens and charges no protocol fee — you pay only Solana’s network gas. Your tokens go into a program you can read on-chain — Jupiter never holds them, and neither do we.

Cliff + vesting schedule

You set a start, an optional cliff, and a vesting duration + interval. Example: lock 10M, cliff 3 months, then vest linearly over 12 months — the schedule is fixed the moment you create it.

Why it builds trust

Locked supply = supply that can’t be dumped. It’s one of the clearest on-chain signals that a team is aligned for the long term — which is exactly why we track and celebrate it for CLKN.

Verify everything

Every lock lives on Solana. See CLKN’s at lock.jup.ag or right here in the Lock of Fame.

Fees & Token-2022 tokens

The cheapest lock there is — and no cut of your tokens

This page runs on Jupiter Lock: zero protocol fees, no percentage of your tokens, ever. You pay only Solana’s network fee plus a small account rent (~0.01 SOL). Most other lockers charge a flat fee and/or a percentage of the tokens you lock — that’s the model we deliberately don’t use.

Want your lock on lock.jup.ag too?

Pursue Jupiter verification for your token separately (real liquidity, metadata, and socials all help the case). Once verified, your token shows in Jupiter’s UI as well — but you never had to wait for that to lock it here.

Is this safe?

No approvals. Nothing to revoke.

Locking here does not grant a token approval, a delegate, or any standing permission on your wallet — the kind of thing that later needs revoking. It’s a single transfer into Jupiter’s program, signed once by you. When it’s done, there’s nothing left on your wallet to take back.

Non-custodial & audited

Your tokens go into Jupiter Lock — an open-source program audited by OtterSec and Sec3 — not a wallet we (or Jupiter) control. We never touch your keys: we build the transaction, you sign it in your own wallet.

The one thing to know

A non-cancelable lock is permanent — that’s the point of it. Choose your schedule and cancelable/permanent setting deliberately before you sign. (Curious about wallet permissions in general? Our free Security Co-op scans and revokes risky approvals on any wallet.)

ⓘ What do these mean?

Cancelable — if on, you (the creator) can cancel the lock and take the tokens back before they finish vesting. Convenient, but it makes the lock far weaker as a trust signal: holders can’t rely on tokens you’re able to pull back. Leave it off for a real, unbreakable “these can’t move” lock.

Recipient can be changed — if on, you can later redirect who claims the vested tokens. Genuinely useful for: vesting to an OTC investor whose wallet may change; or locking from one wallet and claiming from another — so if the original wallet is ever compromised, you can point the lock at a fresh, clean wallet for the long term. Leave it off to make the recipient permanent.
