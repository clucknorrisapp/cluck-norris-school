🎮 SURVIVAL SIMULATOR · WHERE THEORY HITS REALITY

Most crypto education is quizzes. Quizzes test recognition. They don't test the decision — the part where you have $1,000, a DM in your inbox, a green candle, and three seconds to react.

The Survival Simulator gives you that decision. 135 hand‑written scenarios across four categories — each run draws 10 at random, so no two playthroughs are alike:

  • DANGER (47) — friendly devs in DMs, dev wallet dumps, 900% APR honey‑pots, phishing impersonators, wallet drainers, address poisoning, lookalike domains, frozen exchanges, vesting unlock cliffs
  • EMOTIONAL (27) — FOMO buys at the top, panic sells at the bottom, revenge trades, boredom trades, chasing a parabola, the urge to average down on a clear rug
  • KNOWLEDGE (37) — reading Solscan correctly, interpreting tokenomics and FDV, leverage and liquidation, gas and priority fees, distinguishing real airdrops from drainer sites
  • OPPORTUNITY (24) — real alpha when you see it, sizing into conviction, DCA plans, taking profit on the way up, knowing when to LP

You start with $1,000 simulated. Each choice has a real multiplier — disciplined, well‑researched calls grow your stake; reckless ones can wipe the round to near zero. Cluck explains every outcome in plain English. You lose money in the simulator so you don't lose it in real life.

This is the feature that separates the school from a glossary. The lessons teach you what a rug looks like. The simulator teaches you what it feels like to almost click the button, and then choose not to.

💰 PRICING STRATEGY · WHY IT'S 1¢ TODAY (AND WHAT THAT MEANS LATER)

Look at our pricing table — most tools are 1–6¢ in USD terms. Some investors see that and ask "how is this ever real revenue?" Fair question. Here's the play:

  • Aggressive pricing now is a growth choice. The cheaper the unlock, the lower the friction for first‑time users. We'd rather a stranger try the airdropper for $0.01 and tell their friends than charge $1 and never see them. Adoption beats early margin every time.
  • Every tool use sends CLKN on‑chain. Not a "click to use" abstract — actual on‑chain volume tied to actual product activity. The more people use the tools, the more buy pressure on CLKN to refill their wallets.
  • Fees are denominated in CLKN, not USD. Every tool has a fixed price set in CLKN tokens, not dollars — the airdropper, for example, unlocks for a flat 100 CLKN. That number lives in a small server‑side pricing table; it isn't pegged to a dollar amount and doesn't move when the market does. At today's price, 100 CLKN happens to be about $0.01. If the token doubles, that same 100‑CLKN unlock is worth $0.02 to the project — the buyer still pays the same 100 CLKN, the same on‑chain mechanism, but each use brings in double the USD. Fees scale with the token's price automatically; we don't have to change anything.
  • If usage outpaces price, we adjust the table. Pricing lives in a server‑side table (TOOL_GRANTS in server.js) — a one‑line change updates every tool. If 100 CLKN starts feeling free because adoption took off, we raise it. Pricing is a dial we can turn — not a number frozen into a contract.
  • This is the compound effect. Adoption → usage → on‑chain demand → price appreciation → automatic fee scaling → more revenue per use → reinvest in liquidity/locks/development → more credible product → more adoption.
  • The token creator is its own tier. The Hatchery isn't a penny micropayment. It guides you through minting a real SPL token — the standard token format on Solana, the same kind every project on the network is built on — and getting the irreversible decisions right: supply, metadata, and revoking mint and freeze authority. It's a guided creator, not a launchpad: it deliberately stops at the mint and won't add liquidity or open trading for you. That's the point — minting and launching are two separate decisions, and rushing them together in one sitting is exactly how beginners get hurt. The Hatchery makes sure the token is created correctly; when and how to launch stays a deliberate choice you make later. Minting one token is a one-time, higher-value action, so it carries a flat fee (about 0.1 SOL, or the CLKN equivalent at a discount; free for 2M+ CLKN holders) — a real but fair price to create a token properly.

Most token utilities denominate fees in USD and just become more expensive in token terms as price moves. Ours flow the other way — token holders get cheaper relative access as the token they hold appreciates. That's the alignment.

We'd rather be aggressive now and adjust later than gatekeep early adopters out of the system. Brand recognition first. Margin later.
