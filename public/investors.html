Real engineering, not vibes:

  • Frontend: React + Vite for the school; vanilla HTML + JS for the operator tool pages (faster iteration, smaller surface)
  • Backend: Node.js + Express on Railway, MIT open source on GitHub

🎮 SURVIVAL SIMULATOR · WHERE THEORY HITS REALITY

Most crypto education is quizzes. Quizzes test recognition. They don't test the decision — the part where you have $1,000, a DM in your inbox, a green candle, and three seconds to react.

The Survival Simulator gives you that decision. Fifty hand‑written scenarios across four categories:

  • DANGER (21) — friendly devs in DMs, dev wallet dumps, 900% APR honey‑pots, phishing impersonators, vesting unlock cliffs, sudden liquidity removals
  • EMOTIONAL (17) — FOMO buys at the top, panic sells at the bottom, revenge trades, the urge to average down on a clear rug, watching a coin you sold 5× without you
  • KNOWLEDGE (16) — reading Solscan correctly, interpreting tokenomics, evaluating LP locks, distinguishing real airdrops from drainer sites
  • OPPORTUNITY (13) — real alpha when you see it, sizing into conviction, taking profit on the way up, knowing when to LP

You start with $1,000 simulated. Each choice has a real multiplier — the bad ones cost you 80‑95% of the stake. Cluck explains every outcome in plain English. You lose money in the simulator so you don't lose it in real life.

This is the feature that separates the school from a glossary. The lessons teach you what a rug looks like. The simulator teaches you what it feels like to almost click the button, and then choose not to.

💰 PRICING STRATEGY · WHY IT'S 1¢ TODAY (AND WHAT THAT MEANS LATER)

Look at our pricing table — most tools are 1–6¢ in USD terms. Some investors see that and ask "how is this ever real revenue?" Fair question. Here's the play:

  • Aggressive pricing now is a growth choice. The cheaper the unlock, the lower the friction for first‑time users. We'd rather a stranger try the airdropper for $0.01 and tell their friends than charge $1 and never see them. Adoption beats early margin every time.
  • Every tool use sends CLKN on‑chain. Not a "click to use" abstract — actual on‑chain volume tied to actual product activity. The more people use the tools, the more buy pressure on CLKN to refill their wallets.
  • Fees are denominated in CLKN, not USD. A 100‑CLKN airdrop unlock is $0.01 today. If the token doubles, that same unlock is $0.02 — same on‑chain mechanism, double the USD revenue per use. We don't have to do anything for fees to scale with price.
  • If usage outpaces price, we adjust the table. Pricing lives in a server‑side table (TOOL_GRANTS in server.js) — a one‑line change updates every tool. If 100 CLKN starts feeling free because adoption took off, we raise it. We're a knob, not a contract promise.
  • This is the compound effect. Adoption → usage → on‑chain demand → price appreciation → automatic fee scaling → more revenue per use → reinvest in liquidity/locks/development → more credible product → more adoption.
  • The token creator is its own tier. The Hatchery isn't a penny micropayment — minting a real SPL token is a one‑time, higher‑value action, so it carries a flat fee (≈0.1 SOL, or the CLKN equivalent at a discount; free for 2M+ CLKN holders). Pennies for research and operator tools; a real but fair fee to create a token.

Most token utilities denominate fees in USD and just become more expensive in token terms as price moves. Ours flow the other way — token holders get cheaper relative access as the token they hold appreciates. That's the alignment.

We'd rather be aggressive now and adjust later than gatekeep early adopters out of the system. Brand recognition first. Margin later.
